Looking Back, To Look Forward: An ERP Evolution Ghostwritten for Henry Ward, CEO — published on his LinkedIn read the piece

Maybe it’s just that time of the year, but I’ve been thinking a lot about how Carta has evolved, and where we are headed next. I think that’s one difference between Founder CEOs and Professional CEOs. Professional CEOs focus almost entirely on the future, but Founders have watched an idea grow—into something tangible, and then successful—all the while deeply feeling every bump and stumble along the way. It’s a little like being a parent. So proud, yet so protective.

I’ve felt the bumps and stumbles here at Carta over the years. They stung at the time. But, looking back, they now seem like almost inevitable consequences of companies that move fast and iterate often.

There are valuable lessons in looking back. It helps illuminate the path that led to this thrilling new chapter we enter into now.

Carta began with a simple concept—fix the outdated ways of cap table tracking and management, by taking them out of spreadsheets and into software. From there, we marched forward, led by a simple North Star—meet the customers where their needs were, and build products to revolutionize private capital finance, everywhere we could.

So we moved along the line from Startups to Venture, building solutions for fund financials, valuation, tax reporting, PortCo monitoring. And now into Private Equity, leading with waterfall modeling, and LP reporting. [chart in the original post]

But here’s why it can be so valuable to step back, and away from the often myopic obsession with the future. We’ve been so laser focused on these individual solutions, I didn’t fully appreciate that, all along, we’d been building the parts of something far greater in sum—an ERP.

I don’t love the term ERP—it’s dated and vague. “Enterprise Resource Planning” systems started in the ’60s, and came alive in the ’80s and ’90s, for manufacturing and dot-com era software. NetSuite is the classic model today, and one I spend a lot of time exploring.

But it’s essentially the ideal place you want to land as a SaaS company—a fully integrated, all-in-one solution. A one-stop-shop. And that’s what we are building at Carta.

People often ask me what a CEO does. Mind the ship? Lead by example? Hire great people? Sure. But, admittedly, we have such an incredible team here, such an eclectic ensemble of razor-sharp innovators, problem-solvers and leaders, they don’t need me day-to-day to mind the helm.

But what is my job, is to make sure the ship is headed in the right direction, years in advance. And so over this past year I’ve been focused on laying out the scaffolding for how and why Carta fully makes this transition.

I’m overwhelmed with excitement for this new phase of our evolution, from scrappy startup to full-fledged platform. I’ve been on a roadshow to our offices around the world, meeting with our teams to talk through how this ERP vision is coming together. And we made a short explainer video here that gives a high-level picture of what this looks like.

I can’t wait to share more in this new year…

Thank you — happy to walk through any of these.
